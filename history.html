History and Key Achievements of Human Space Activities

First Human in Space

On 12 April 1961, Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human to journey into outer space, completing one orbit of Earth aboard Vostok 1 in 108 minutes.

Moon Landing

On 20 July 1969, NASA's Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

Establishment of the International Space Station

The ISS assembly began in 1998. It is a multinational collaborative project and has been continuously occupied since November 2000, serving as a laboratory for science and technology.

Recent Mars Exploration Missions

Rovers such as Curiosity, Perseverance, and the Zhurong rover have explored the Martian surface. Orbital missions like ESA's Mars Express and NASA's MAVEN continue to study the planet.

First Spacewalk

On 18 March 1965, Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov performed the first extravehicular activity (EVA), leaving the Voskhod 2 spacecraft for about 12 minutes.
